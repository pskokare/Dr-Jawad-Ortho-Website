UNDERSTANDING CARTILAGE

What is a cartilage?

Cartilage tissue in joints is always found where movements occur. Cartilage is an elastic tissue that covers bone in area of joint and acts as a buffer.

Nature of cartilage damage

Sometimes there is a thinning of cartilage in a small to a medium-sized area of joint. This can be caused by injuries, over- or under-loading. But cartilage damage can also occur for no apparent reason, such as in so-called. Osteochondritis dissecans. In this disease, a circumscribed bone/cartilage part dies in joint and result is pain. Sometimes, cartilage damage occurs even after so-called accompanying injuries such as meniscal damage or cruciate ligament tears. Malformations of joint may also be responsible for development of cartilage damage. For example, an O-leg often leads to cartilage damage on the inside of knee joint. Of circumscribed cartilage defects mentioned above, those that affect entire joint are to be delineated.

THE PROCEDURE

Cartilage cell transplantation in Hyderabad

With introduction of cartilage cell transplantation, it was possible for the first time to achieve a cartilage-like replacement cartilage defect. Microscopic examination clearly shows that tissue that results from cartilage cell transplantation is most similar to original cartilage compared to other therapies. In cartilage cell transplantation, cartilage is removed from those parts of the affected joint that are otherwise less stressed during arthroscopy. The amounts that are taken are very small (about the size of a small fingernail) and do not affect the joint. The cells in this piece of cartilage are then cultivated under unique safety and purity conditions in the laboratory and multiplied to several million cells. The cells are grown on different carrier materials. After a few weeks, this fleece can then be introduced into the defect with cartilage cells. Usually only a very small incision is necessary.

Recovery time

After operation, the affected joint is protected when walking with a splint, and the joint can also bear little to no load for 6-8 weeks. The use of crutches is needed for a month. Intensive physiotherapy is necessary for recuperation, and after 2-3 months the joint is as good as new.
